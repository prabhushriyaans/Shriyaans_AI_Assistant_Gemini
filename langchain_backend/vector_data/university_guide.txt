Study Abroad Guide:
- Top countries: USA, Canada, UK, Germany, Australia, Ireland, France, Netherlands, New Zealand, Singapore
- Required tests: IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, GRE, GMAT, SAT, ACT, LSAT, MCAT
- Documents: SOP, LORs, Resume/CV, Academic Transcripts, Degree Certificates, Passport, Financial Statements, Application Forms
- Visa requirements:
  - USA (F-1 visa): I-20 form, SEVIS fee, visa interview
  - Canada (Study Permit): GIC proof, medical exam, biometrics
  - UK (Student Visa): CAS letter, healthcare surcharge, financial proof
  - Germany (Student Visa): €11,208 in blocked account, admission letter
  - Australia (Subclass 500): GTE statement, proof of funds, health insurance
- Scholarships available:
  - International: DAAD (Germany), Fulbright (USA), Chevening (UK), Erasmus Mundus (EU), Australia Awards, Vanier (Canada)
  - Country-Specific:
    - USA: Hubert Humphrey Fellowship, Knight-Hennessy, AAUW (for women)
    - UK: Commonwealth Scholarships, Rhodes (Oxford)
    - Canada: Trudeau Foundation, Ontario Graduate Scholarships
    - Germany: Heinrich Böll Foundation, Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung
    - France: Eiffel Excellence Scholarships
    - Netherlands: Holland Scholarship, Orange Tulip Scholarship
  - Types: Merit-based, Need-based, University-specific, Government-funded
- Part-time work opportunities:
  - USA: 20 hrs/week on-campus, OPT (12–36 months)
  - Canada: 20 hrs/week off-campus, PGWP up to 3 years
  - UK: 20 hrs/week, Graduate Route Visa (2–3 years)
  - Germany: 120 full days/year, 18-month job seeker visa
  - Australia: 40 hrs per fortnight, PSW visa (2–4 years)
- Cost of living & tuition (per year, approx.):
  - USA: Tuition $20,000–$50,000 | Living $12,000–$18,000
  - Canada: Tuition CAD 15,000–25,000 | Living CAD 10,000–15,000
  - UK: Tuition £12,000–25,000 | Living £9,000–12,000
  - Germany: Tuition Free–€5,000 | Living €8,000–12,000
  - Australia: Tuition AUD 20,000–40,000 | Living AUD 15,000–20,000
- Career & PR pathways:
  - USA: H-1B visa after OPT (competitive lottery)
  - Canada: Express Entry PR after PGWP + work experience
  - UK: Skilled Worker visa after Graduate Route visa
  - Germany: EU Blue Card after studies + job offer
  - Australia: Points-based PR system, education adds points
